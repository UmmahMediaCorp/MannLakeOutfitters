# Mann Lake Outfitters — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Hunting & fishing outfitter in east-central Saskatchewan, Canada. Brand assets: bold black-on-white woodcut-style logo with deer/pines. Affiliated lodge: Costigan Lake Lodge (rustic brown branding). Photos: trophy Northern Pike, Lake Trout, Black Bear hunts, dramatic lake sunsets.

**Idea 1: Rugged Timber & Slate**

- **Design Movement**: Arts & Crafts / National Parks Poster aesthetic
- **Core Principles**: Raw texture, hand-crafted feel, deep wilderness atmosphere, bold typographic hierarchy
- **Color Philosophy**: Deep forest greens (oklch 0.28 0.08 145), warm amber/gold (oklch 0.72 0.15 75), slate charcoal (oklch 0.18 0.01 260), cream parchment (oklch 0.95 0.02 85). Evokes campfire, pine sap, and dawn mist.
- **Layout Paradigm**: Full-bleed photo sections with diagonal cuts between them. Asymmetric text blocks offset to left or right. Sticky nav with transparent-to-solid scroll transition.
- **Signature Elements**: Woodcut-style SVG dividers between sections; weathered texture overlay on hero; stamp/badge UI for season dates and species info.
- **Interaction Philosophy**: Parallax scrolling on hero photo. Hover states reveal info cards with a "lift" shadow. Page transitions feel like turning a field journal page.
- **Animation**: Subtle fade-up on scroll for content blocks. Logo entrance with a slow reveal. Nav links underline draws from left.
- **Typography System**: Playfair Display (serif, bold) for headings — evokes classic hunting lodge signage. Source Sans 3 (humanist sans) for body. Tight tracking on all-caps subheadings.

## Selected Design: **Idea 1 — Rugged Timber & Slate**

This approach best honors the Mann Lake Outfitters brand identity — the woodcut-style logo, the Saskatchewan wilderness setting, and the authentic hunting/fishing heritage. The Arts & Crafts / National Parks aesthetic creates a premium yet deeply authentic feel that will resonate with serious hunters and anglers.
